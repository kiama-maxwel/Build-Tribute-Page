MR. JOMO KENYATTA

The man who saved Kenyans

Jomo Kenyatta CGH ( c. 1897 – 22 August 1978) was a Kenyan anti-colonial activist, revolutionary, political theorist, cultural theorist and politician who governed Kenya as its Prime Minister from 1963 to 1964 and then as its first President from 1964 to his death in 1978.

Here's a time line of Mr. Kenyatta's life:

1897 Born in Nairobi ,Kenya

1929 he travelled to London to lobby for Kikuyu land affairs

1930 he studied at Moscow's Communist University of the Toilers of the East, University College London,

1938 he published an anthropological study of Kikuyu life

1946 He returned to Kenya and became a school principal

1952 he was among the Kapenguria Six arrested and charged with masterminding the anti-colonial Mau Mau Uprising.

1961 he was realised from prison and set free

1963 Kenyatta became President of KANU and led the party to victory

1964 He became the first president of the republic of Kenya

1966 He made several constitutional amendments enhanced the president's power

1969 Kenyatta visited Kisumu, located in Luo territory, to open a hospital.

1970 He was increasingly feeble and senile and For many years, Kenyatta had suffered health problems

1975 Kenyatta had—according to Maloba—"in effect ceased to actively govern".

1977 Kenyatta had several further strokes or heart attacks

1978 he died of a heart attack in the State House, Mombasa-Kenya

"He was the country's first president and played a significant role in the transformation
of Kenya from a colony of the British Empire into an independent republic.
Ideologically an African nationalist and a conservative, he led the Kenya African
National Union (KANU) party from 1961 until his death." -Maxwel Kiama
